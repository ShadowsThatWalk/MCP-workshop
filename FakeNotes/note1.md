# Building a House

A concise, test-friendly note outlining the typical stages and decisions involved in building a house.

## Background

This note models a small residential construction project so tooling can be exercised against realistic phases, checklists, and references.

## Goals

- Produce a clear, staged plan that can be parsed by tooling
- Provide sample headings, nested lists, and action items for tests

## Milestones

### Phase 1 — Planning & Design

- Site survey and soil testing
- Architectural plans and material selection
- Secure permits and approvals

### Phase 2 — Foundations & Structure

- Excavation and grading
- Pour foundation (slab, crawlspace, or basement)
- Framing: walls, floors, roof

### Phase 3 — Systems & Finishes

- Install plumbing, electrical, and HVAC
- Insulation, drywall, and exterior finishes
- Paint, flooring, and fixtures

### Phase 4 — Inspection & Handover

- Final building inspection and certificates
- Punchlist completion
- Handover to owner and documentation

## References

- Local building codes and permit offices
- Standard material checklist
